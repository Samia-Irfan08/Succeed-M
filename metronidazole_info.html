Metronidazole: Comprehensive Information

1. Basic Information

  • Generic Name: Metronidazole
  • Brand Names: Flagyl, Metrogel, Metrogyl, etc.
  • Class: Nitroimidazole antibiotic and antiprotozoal
  • Routes: Oral, IV, Topical, Vaginal

2. Chemical & Physical Properties

  • Formula: C₆H₉N₃O₃
  • Weight: 171.16 g/mol
  • Solubility: Water, alcohol
  • Half-life: 6–8 hours

3. Mechanism of Action

Metronidazole is reduced by nitroreductase enzymes in anaerobes to active metabolites that damage DNA and cause cell death.

4. Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Effective against anaerobes: Bacteroides, Clostridium
  • Protozoa: Entamoeba, Giardia, Trichomonas

6. Adult Dosage

7. Pediatric Dose

30–50 mg/kg/day in divided doses every 8 hours. Max: adult dose.

8. Side Effects

9. Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity
  • First trimester of pregnancy (trichomoniasis)
  • Alcohol use (disulfiram reaction)

10. Drug Interactions

11. Pregnancy & Lactation

Category B; avoid in 1st trimester. Excreted in milk; avoid or pause breastfeeding after high doses.

12. Pharmacokinetics

  • Well absorbed orally
  • Distributed to CSF and abscesses
  • Metabolized in liver
  • Excreted via urine/feces

13. Storage

  • Tablets: Room temperature
  • IV: Protect from light
  • Topical: Below 25°C
